The Generalized Entropy Index (GE) is a measure of inequality in a population. Rather than considering income, however, in our analysis we look at learning outcomes as represented by oral reading fluency scores in English and Kiswahili.

GE is parametrized by $\alpha$. When using these EGRA data, zero-scores (children who were unable to read a single word) are prevalent. It is therefore necessary to use a version of the GE function that does not discard zero-valued inputs (as is the case with GE(0), GE(1), GE(-1)). We therefore applied GE(2), which is equivalent to half the squared coefficient of variation.1

GE(2) can be additively decomposed into within-group and between-group inequality. The table below shows the GE(2) for various groups within the Tusome EGRA data.
